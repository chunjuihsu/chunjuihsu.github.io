How did International K-Pop Fans React to Multinational K-Pop Group?

What can sound more contradictory than a K-Pop group based not in Korea, having no Korean members, and singing mostly in English? Multinational K-Pop has been controversial in the culture for a long time, but now their numbers are increasing. In this project, I focused on six Multinational K-Pop groups and discussed what aspects of Multinational K-Pop did K-Pop fans concern the most about, through an analysis of comments collected from these groups' music videos on YouTube.

Research Objective

  1. What aspects of a Multinational K-Pop group did K-Pop fans concern about? What is their reaction?
  2. Whether it is a good idea for an investor to invest Multinational K-Pop now?
  3. If someone wants to promote a Multinational K-Pop group, what should he or she pay the most attention to?

Research Subjects

  1. Exp Edition, consisting of four white Americans
  2. UHSN, consisting of ten members from ten different countries
  3. 5High, consisting of five Indians
  4. KAACHI, consisting of three girls from the UK and one girl from Korea
  5. Black Swan, consisting of three Korean, one Senegalese, and one Brazilian
  6. Prisma, consisting of two Korean, one American, one Italian, and one Spanish

Data Collection

  1. Data collection was done through Google API. Parameter Order was set to relevance, which means the API will fetch the most relevant comments based on the Google algorithm. Both comments and replies were fetched.
  2. Data collection was done on October 15, 2021.
  3. More than twenty thousand comments were collected from nine YouTube videos at the end of the data collection.

Key Findings

  1. Promoting Multinational K-Pop is still a good strategy to attract attention as the discussion about Multinational K-Pop did not seem to cease in the last few months.
  2. This analysis provides a robust prediction model for Multinational K-Pop group managers to monitor any future promotion.
  3. The result helps identify the aspect of existing Multinational K-Pop groups that need to be worked on more to satisfy their viewers.

Result Four: What Aspects of a Multinational K-Pop group did K-Pop Fans Concern About?

The following analysis, focusing only on comments written in English, due to the researcher's literacy, was based on the result of a classification model using TF-IDF and Naive Bayes. Three mutually inclusive variables were made after initial data exploration. These variables are comments about the quality of the K-Pop group (quality), nationalist or ethnicist comments (nationalist-ethnicist), and comments that argue about what K-Pop is (k-pop).

  1. This figure presents the mean TF-IDF score of each word scored in the process for the model to predict whether a sentence is a comment about K-Pop or not.
  2. It can be seen the model works quite well as words that refer to different aspects of a K-Pop group, such as asian, english, and american were as expected on the left-hand figure and not on the right-hand one.
  1. This figure presents the mean TF-IDF score of each word scored in the process for the model to predict whether a sentence is a nationalist/ethnicist comment or not.
  2. From the words placed on the left-hand figure but not on the right-hand figure, we found some unique words that are predicted as words describing K-Pop. These words are culture and industry.
  3. This model also shows how the concept of K-Pop is intercepted with ideas about the performative quality and nationalist/ethnicist background of a K-Pop group, but such inference requires further analysis, which will be presented as follows.
  1. This figure represents the connections between concepts that were identified based on the result of the prediction models. Each node represents a concept, which consists of a lexicon that links to this concept (e.g. voice and vocal as the lexicon of voice). The width of edges that link different nodes represents the count of the co-occurrence of the two concepts in the same sentences. The thicker edge indicates more co-occurrence.
  2. The figure shows that K-Pop had a strong association with concepts about quality in volume. K-Pop particularly co-occurred with the idea of song many times, implying songs are an important part of K-Pop to most K-Pop fans.
  3. For nationalist and ethnicist concepts, the idea of Asians was associated with K-Pop more than others.
  4. While this figure only provides frequencies of two given concepts that were co-occurred, this figure did not present whether it was a positive association or a negative association. For example, "K-Pop does not need to be Asian." and "K-Pop is for Asian!!!" could mean very different things, but both occurrences will be calculated in this network.
  5. Therefore, this chapter concludes that the performative quality of K-Pop is more important than the K-Pop's nationalist or ethnicist background. Nevertheless, how fans actually felt about these connections will require further sentiment analysis to tell.

Result Five: An Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis of Multinational K-Pop

The following sentiment analysis used a pre-trained lexicon- and rule-based sentiment analyzer Vader to determine the sentiment score at the scale of a sentence. It focused on aspects rather than a general sentiment score, so the result can be more insightful for group managers to identify the problems and design business strategies accordingly.

  1. The figure shows the proportions of different sentiments reflected in the sentences about quality under music videos of different Multinational K-Pop groups.
  2. The result shows that 5 High had the greatest proportion of negative sentiment, around 23.9%, followed by KAACHI's 20.4%, suggesting there was a huge concern about their quality.
  3. Meanwhile, UHSN, Prisma and Black Swan had a smaller proportion of negative sentiment about their quality, suggesting fans were satisfied with their music and performance.
  1. This figure shows the proportion of negative sentiment about different aspects of quality. The X-axis represents the percentage of a given negative sentiment toward a specific quality aspect occupied in the total negative sentiment about quality. The different colors of the circle represent the different aspects the circles represent, and the size of the circle indicates the count of such occurrences.
  2. The result shows that songs are the main concern most commenters had about these multinational K-Pop groups, suggesting a place where the group managers can work more in their next release.
  3. Vocal ability appeared to be a big problem for 5 High, as almost 40% of the negative sentiment was about it.
  4. Black Swan and Prisma performed relatively well regarding their language, dance, and vocal ability. It can be seen from the small circular shapes that represent the count of negative sentiments toward these qualities.
  1. The figure presents the proportion of different sentiments reflected in the sentences having a nationalist and ethnicist utterance under music videos of Multinational K-Pop groups.
  2. The result shows that KAACHI had the greatest proportion of negative sentiment, around 32.9%, followed by UHSN's 30%, suggesting there was a big concern about their nationalist and ethnicist background.
  3. Meanwhile, more than 47% of the sentiment with a nationalist and ethnicist utterance under the music videos of 5 High and Prisma were positive, suggesting their nationalist-ethnicist backgrounds appealed to commenters.
  1. This figure presents the proportion of different sentiments about different aspects of nationalist-ethnicist utterance. The X-axis represents the percentage of a given sentiment occupied in all utterances, and the Y-axis represents different nationalist-ethnicist aspects. The different colors of the circle represent the different sentiments, and the size of the circular shape indicates the count of such occurrences.
  2. The result shows that Indian was the nationalist-ethnicist concept the most likely to be positively addressed. Around 48 percent of the utterances were addressed positively.
  3. Meanwhile, White was the concept that was the most likely to be negatively addressed. About 35% of the sentences that mentioned White were negatively addressed.
  4. In general, racial issues are topics likely to be addressed negatively by K-Pop fans, but Black, Indian, and Asian appeared to be safer situations than White.

Conclusion

  1. Considering the unceasing discussion about Multinational K-Pop, this analysis suggested promoting Multinational K-Pop is an effective strategy for newly promoted K-Pop groups to gain attention.
  2. The analysis found that the concept of K-Pop was more linked to its performative quality instead of its nationalist and ethnicist backgrounds. The result suggested that most K-Pop fans were concerned about Multinational K-Pop groups' performative quality rather than their nationalist and ethnicist backgrounds.
  3. The analysis identified the performative aspects of Multinational K-Pop groups that need to be worked on more to satisfy their viewers. Songs are the shared aspect that most of them will need to improve. 5 High needs to improve their vocal ability, and KAACHI might need to start with their dancing and vocal ability.
  4. The prediction models were robust, so they can be used to facilitate future promotions of Multinational K-Pop groups.
  5. Ideally, a Multinational K-Pop manager would like to have more viewers focusing on the group's performative aspects rather than their nationalist-ethnicist backgrounds because nationalist-ethnicist comments were more likely to generate negative sentiments.
  6. Nevertheless, Black and Indian K-Pop idols appeared to be a safer choice than White idols because of the greater proportion of positive sentiments associated with Black and Indian.
  7. The analysis also found that the members' nationality helps generate traffics from the national market of that given member.
  8. Therefore, this project proposed that a non-White idol, who is from a country with a large population and consumption power, would be an ideal option for the next Multinational K-Pop to promote.
