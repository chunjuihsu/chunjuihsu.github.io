Project 2: Do Your Team Managers Communicate Well with Their Team Members?

A Korean Company, C, has implemented a manager-member communication scheme for the last four years. This scheme requires team managers to write monthly performance reports for individual team members. The scheme has accumulated more than 50 thousand records of performance reports and an immense amount of text. The chair of Company C wants to see how well team managers communicate with the employees with this scheme. Previously, it relied on the in-house survey that asks employees how satisfied they are with this scheme, but this didn't work very well because Korean culture discourages employees from displaying their dissatisfaction. The HR team occasionally looked up the performance reports manually and briefed the chair, but the big amount of data made this task inefficient and lacking holistic insight.

I did this project in a team of two. My task was to help the HR team develop a data-based evaluation strategy that examines how well the team managers perform communication under this scheme. Using data analysis techniques and NLP methods, I generated five features concerning any given team manager and the performance reports written. These five features are the length of individual report, timeframe similarity, between-member similarity, manager-self similarity, and omission count. With these five features, we developed a dashboard with SPOTFIRE, visualizing these features in an easy-to-grasp manner.

Feedback from the HR team was positive. Although the HR team had some concerns about the utility of some features, they liked the visualization and thought it helps enhance their work efficiency and provides unique insights that are otherwise impossible to acquire. For myself, I wish I could make the program updatable, so the program can still be functional when new data arrive in the future.

Data Visualization

In this section, I present some visualization works using data from the World COVID-19 Pandemic and Citizen Life Survey, part of a research project that I participated in when I was a graduate research assistant at Seoul National University.

Which city got freaked out the most if someone is not wearing a face mask?

While wearing a face mask was considered the most effective way to prevent the virus from transmitting, let's look at which city got freaked out the most if someone is not wearing a face mask and which city was the most relaxed about it.

How did cities think about making patients' footprint information public?

Making public the patients' past footprint was one way to contain the virus. Some people think it is an infringement of privacy, but others think the citizens have the right to be informed. With this figure, we see how different political ideology camps in different cities had different patterns in seeing this controversial pandemic management policy.

If you are infected, are you more worried about yourself or the consequent impact on your family?

COVID-19 is dangerous. There is so far no effective treatment, and it is also highly contagious. In this figure, we see cities were segregated into one cluster of self-oriented pandemic anxiety and the other cluster of family-oriented pandemic anxiety.
